Designing an AI agent that helps marketers write content that ranks

This project focused on designing an enriched SEO Agent that helps content marketers produce articles that are substantive enough to rank, transparent enough to trust, and structured enough to publish without major edits.

  • 3 rounds of usability testing
  • Working AI agent built for testing
  • Prototype shipped in production codebase

As a product designer on the team, I led the UX and UI design of the SEO agent while collaborating with a lead product designer, a product manager, and a lead user researcher.

Project Scope

Brief

SEO-optimized pages were the top requested AI use case

The UX research team spoke with 15 content marketers. Companies were already investing in content through freelancers and publishing tools. The pages still weren't ranking.

Existing AI tools didn't help. You enter a keyword and get an article back, with no way to know why it was written that way or whether it would work.

Understanding workflow

One marketer used 18 agents just to get a decent article

When I interviewed one of our internal content marketers, I expected a simple workflow. Instead, he showed me 18 agents in his Clay setup. Each one handled a different piece of the SEO process. The result was an article he actually trusted.

The other marketer relied on a freelancer and manual review in Notion. A different process but still with the same problem: not wanting to spend hours correcting an article after the fact.

A marketer's Clay workspace: 18 chained power agents to produce one SEO article

Opportunity

Give marketers control before the AI writes, not after

Hypothesis

Marketers would rather spend time making sure the AI has all the information it needs upfront than receive a full article and spend hours reading and correcting it.

The SEO agent breaks content creation into three transparent steps:

Research → Outline → Article

How I worked

Prototyping with AI, not just for AI

I wanted every test to feel real and moved away from static Figma mockups.

So I built a working SEO agent in Langchain's Agent Builder. It analysed competitors, identified content gaps, and generated outlines from real data. The first test was run on actual AI output. It confirmed that marketers wanted an agent they could work with.

For the interface, I replicated Prismic's UI in Lovable. Our marketers could freely interact with a prototype that felt embedded in a live product. As a result feedback got sharper and I was able to iterate at a faster pace.

When the design was validated, I used Claude Code to rebuild the prototype directly in Prismic's codebase. Engineering could build from working code instead of interpreting static specs.

Design Exploration 1

Competitive research went from a background step to the most important one

I built an agent that analyses top-ranking pages for a given keyword, identifies what they miss, and generates a structured article from those gaps.

The first test was informal. I sat with Tosin, one of Prismic's content marketers, and showed him the raw output with no interface.

I expected him to skip straight to the outline. He actually spent most of the session reading the research. Like what pages had the agent looked at. Or what questions competitors were failing to answer. He wanted to evaluate the sources before trusting anything the agent had written.

"I can see all the steps it is taking in a granular level. It gives me the confidence that AI did its job. I would say what I would want is the chance to kind of look at those things, being able to edit and add stuff."

Tosin A.

Design Exploration 2

Good links don't come last

Adding internal and external link suggestions to the outline

Both users flagged link-building as something they knew mattered but rarely had time for.

I initially put link suggestions at the end of the flow, after the article was generated just like in Clay. Testing showed this was too late. The structure was already set and adding links at that point changed nothing.

I moved them into the outline step. Before the article is written, users can see suggested internal and external links per section and choose what to keep.

Marketer reviewing the outline after being given task during user test

"I really like that [table for internal linking]. Normally I would have to remember what content I had or just go back and double check what other articles I had that would be relevant to put in. And I would hope the more blog articles I create with this SEO agent, the more comprehensive this list is."

Miranda W.

Current Status

It's in development!

The SEO agent's future

The feature is currently being built by the engineering team. For smoother development handover, I replicated the fully interactive prototype directly in the Prismic codebase using Claude Code.

Testing raised one open question: how to update content that already exists.

Measuring success

The metric to watch post-launch: how much time marketers spend between starting an article and hitting publish. If the agent is doing its job, that number should drop significantly without sacrificing the quality of what ranks.
